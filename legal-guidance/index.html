The Reality

Every provider carries legal exposure they can’t see until it lands at their door.

A RAC audit letter. A patient complaint to the state board. A commercial payer pulling back six months of payments. A documentation detail that, in retrospect, wasn’t worded defensively enough. Most providers learn about the risk after it materializes — when the stakes are highest and the options are fewest.

That’s backwards. We flipped it. Our in-house medical-legal counsel sits inside your relationship with us. They review documentation standards before an audit ever arrives. They shape how treatment decisions are recorded before a patient complaint ever surfaces. They counsel on every gray area so nothing your practice does is done without informed legal backing.

What We Protect Against

The full surface of medical-legal exposure.

The matters our counsel handles for clients — proactively, and when events do arise. Nothing is outsourced. Nothing is re-billed.

Government payer audits

RAC, UPIC, CERT, SMRC, ZPIC and CMS-directed audits. We prepare before they arrive and defend when they do.

Commercial payer audits

Post-payment reviews, recoupment demands, pre-payment flags, and network-termination disputes from private insurers.

Patient complaints & lawsuits

Board complaints, HIPAA grievances, malpractice inquiries, and the defensive positioning that makes them resolvable.

Denial appeals

Every denial reviewed with legal framing. Appeals drafted to the level of a demand letter — not a provider explaining themselves.

Prior-authorization disputes

When payers reject or delay prior auths improperly, we escalate with legal weight on your behalf.

Licensure & credentialing

State board inquiries, credentialing disputes, licensure defense, and proactive compliance documentation.

Stark Law & Anti-Kickback

Arrangement reviews, safe-harbor structuring, and compliance vetting for referral relationships and vendor contracts.

HIPAA & privacy

Breach response, OCR inquiries, policy review, and day-to-day privacy guidance for your team.

DEA & controlled substances

Inspection preparation, prescribing compliance, and defense of DEA inquiries or administrative actions.

Contract review

Payer contracts, employment agreements, vendor and supplier terms — every contract reviewed before signature.

False Claims Act matters

Whistleblower inquiries, self-disclosure strategy, and compliance-program construction that heads them off.

Corporate compliance

OIG-aligned compliance programs, internal policy review, training, and ongoing program maintenance.

How Legal Embeds

Guidance at every step — each patient, each note.

Legal isn’t a phone number you call in a crisis. It’s a quiet current running under every clinical and business decision.

Documentation & note guidance

Every documentation template your practice uses is reviewed for audit defensibility. Notes are structured to survive scrutiny the first time — not reconstructed under pressure months later.

Pre-submission claim review

High-risk claims and repeat-pattern submissions are reviewed before they leave. We catch exposure at the point where it’s free to fix.

Clinical decision sounding board

Gray-area judgment calls — coverage boundaries, off-label situations, prior-auth disputes — reviewed on demand. Not every question needs an attorney; the ones that do, have one immediately.

Emergency response (24-hour)

When an audit letter, subpoena, or complaint arrives, you call us — not an outside firm. Response within 24 hours. Often within the hour.

Policy & procedure construction

Internal policies written and reviewed to align with OIG, CMS, state board, and payer expectations — tailored to your specialty and jurisdiction.

Contract & agreement review

Every payer contract, employment agreement, lease, and vendor contract reviewed before signature. No contract goes live without legal eyes on it.
