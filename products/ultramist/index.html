How it works

Acoustic energy, delivered through a saline mist.

Low-frequency (40 kHz), non-thermal, non-contact ultrasound. The acoustic waves mechanically stimulate cells in and below the wound surface, supporting the body's natural healing cascade in three measurable ways.

Effect 01

Decrease bacteria

Acoustic disruption reduces bacterial load and biofilm at the wound surface, lowering infection-related barriers to healing.

Effect 02

Decrease inflammation

Mechanical stimulation modulates the local inflammatory response, helping chronic wounds progress past the stalled inflammatory phase.

Effect 03

Increase perfusion

Improved blood flow and angiogenic signaling support granulation tissue formation and oxygen delivery to the wound bed.

Reference: Serena TE. Ostomy Wound Management. 2009;55(1):22–30. UltraMIST is an adjunctive treatment for acute and chronic wounds — including over exposed tendon, bone, arteries, veins, metal hardware, staples, and skin grafts. Contraindicated over cancer lesions, electrical implants (e.g., pacemakers), and the pregnant uterus.

Eligibility — at least one required

Per Sanuwave’s reimbursement guide and Medicare LCD guidance, UltraMIST may be utilized when:

  • No significant improvement after 30 days of standard wound care (SOC)
  • Too painful for sharp or excisional debridement
  • Contraindicated for sharp debridement — e.g., unstageable DTI, blood thinners, hemophilia, non-compliant patient

Per-session documentation

Each session should capture all five elements. A qualifying wound diagnosis is required.

  • Pain level
  • Wound measurements — length, width, depth, plus any undermining or tunneling
  • Tissue appearance — granulation vs. necrotic tissue
  • Inflammation / redness
  • Drainage

Reassessment rule: Improvement in at least one of the five documentation elements must be evident by the 6th treatment to continue therapy under medical necessity.

CMS designates 97610 as a “sometimes therapy” code. Furnished under a therapy plan of care (POC), therapy modifiers may be required for MPFS payment; outside a POC, modifiers are typically not required for hospital outpatients. Consult your MAC or payer for state-specific LCD guidance.

Common questions

What to know about UltraMist®.

What can UltraMist treat? +
Sanuwave UltraMist Therapy has been clinically proven to promote healing across a wide range of chronic and acute wounds, including venous leg ulcers, diabetic foot ulcers, deep tissue pressure injuries, surgical wounds, soft tissue injuries, and more. The non-contact ultrasound energy penetrates into and below the wound bed, stimulating cellular activity and promoting tissue repair at the vascular and cellular level.
Is the treatment painful? +
UltraMist treatment is non-contact and painless. Because the ultrasound energy is delivered through a saline mist without touching the wound surface, patients experience no discomfort during treatment. This makes UltraMist particularly appropriate for patients with pain-sensitive wound presentations or those who may have difficulty tolerating contact-based therapies.
How long does treatment take? +
Treatment takes between 3–20 minutes depending on wound size. This efficient treatment window integrates easily into existing wound care visit schedules without creating workflow disruption or extended appointment times for clinicians or patients.
