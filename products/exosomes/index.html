Common questions

What to know about regenerative biologics.

What are exosomes? +
In a wound that has stalled, the underlying problem is signaling — inflammation stays switched on and rebuilding never starts. Exosomes are the signaling packets cells use to change that. These nanoscale vesicles (30–150 nm) deliver growth factors, cytokines, and nucleic acids that help quiet the inflammatory loop, stimulate new blood vessel formation (angiogenesis), and support matrix remodeling and re-epithelialization. And because exosome products are acellular — vesicles, not living cells — storage, handling, and regulatory considerations are simpler than cell-based therapies.
What birth tissue products are available? +
Our regenerative portfolio includes exosome products (NTA-validated for concentration, particle size, and batch consistency), Wharton's Jelly MSC-derived therapies, and fresh-processed placental biologics. All products are processed in ISO 5 classified clean rooms with 24/7 environmental monitoring, inside an FDA-registered cGMP facility. Every batch ships fresh — never frozen — preserving maximum biological activity.
What are the clinical applications? +
Regenerative biologics support a range of wound care and musculoskeletal protocols — including chronic wound healing, inflammation modulation, soft tissue regeneration, and combination therapy alongside allografts and collagen programs. Albacete MedDev provides full operational and compliance infrastructure — documentation support, coding guidance, storage and handling protocols, and clinical education — so your practice can implement these therapies with confidence.
