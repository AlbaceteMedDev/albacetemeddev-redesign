Three Principles

How the advisory operates.

Three commitments that sit behind every recommendation we hand your leadership team.

01 · Payer-aligned

Reimbursement is the filter, not an afterthought

Every recommendation gets tested against payer policy before it reaches your desk. If it doesn’t pay clean, it isn’t a strategy — it’s a write-off waiting to happen. Coding, modifier usage, documentation standards, and LCD alignment are built into the advice from the first conversation.

02 · Operation-grounded

Strategy that survives the first Tuesday

A plan that reads well in a deck and falls apart in the clinic isn’t a plan. Our recommendations are shaped by staff capacity, patient flow, your EHR, your billing system, and the actual cadence of your week — not by an idealized reference architecture.

03 · Specialty-tuned

Built for wound care, not adapted to it

Wound care, podiatry, plastic surgery, vascular, surgical applications — each specialty has its own payer dynamics, documentation expectations, and formulary logic. Advisory is tuned per specialty, not bent toward a generic mean that fits nobody well.

The Advisory Surface

Twelve places strategic advisory pays for itself.

The matters our advisors work through with leadership — across coding, operations, formulary, market access, and growth.

CPT & HCPCS strategy

Per-product, per-payer coding strategy tuned to your specialty — not a generic crosswalk. Modifier sequencing, unit billing, and revenue capture reviewed at the claim level.

Denial root-cause analysis

Claim denials triaged to find the underlying driver — coding, documentation, prior-auth, benefit verification, or payer policy shift — with remediation plans attached.

Benefit verification workflow

Design of the pre-treatment BV workflow so denied coverage is caught before product ships — not after the patient is treated and the claim is on the floor.

Patient-flow optimization

Schedule design, room turn, and staff utilization reviewed against clinical throughput — removing the hidden bottlenecks that cap your weekly case volume.

Staff utilization & roles

Scope-of-practice review, task delegation, and role design so clinical staff work at the top of their license and administrative load doesn’t land on providers.

Formulary design

Specialty-specific product portfolio built around your patient population, payer mix, and clinical focus — not around the deals a distributor is trying to clear.

Onboarding & clinical education

Structured onboarding plans per product line, with clinical education tuned to the nurses, PAs, and physicians who will actually be running the protocol.

Service-line growth

Strategic planning for new wound care service lines — new specialty verticals, new referral pathways, new product categories — grounded in local market dynamics.

Payer coverage analysis

Coverage posture across Medicare, Medicaid, and commercial — with visibility into where your mix is strong, where it’s exposed, and where a policy shift is coming.

Reimbursement pathway design

Designing the reimbursement pathway for new products, new service lines, or new indications — before the first claim is submitted.

Compliance framework

OIG-aligned compliance programs, documentation standards, and audit-preparation protocols tuned to wound care’s specific regulatory surface.

Audit readiness

Pre-audit chart reviews, documentation-pattern monitoring, and risk flagging — so when a RAC, UPIC, or commercial audit lands, your chart stack is already defensible.
